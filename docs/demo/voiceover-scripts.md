# Voiceover scripts — DX walkthrough demos

These scripts pair with the videos produced by `scripts/record_demo.py`.
Each scene's timing matches the `time.sleep(...)` values in that script,
so you can record voiceover against the on-screen captions.

**Pace target:** ~150 words per minute (≈2.5 words / second). Each scene's
narration is calibrated to fit comfortably inside its on-screen window with
a beat to breathe at the end.

**Tone:** confident, dry, factual. No "AI-powered." No "leverage." No
exclamation marks. The page already pitches; the voiceover lets the work
speak.

**Recording note:** the captions on screen carry the headline. Your voice
adds the *why* — the partner-grade context the captions don't have room for.
Don't read the captions back; talk over them.

### Scene 8 — Outro (0:46–0:51, 5s)

> "Three demos, three real public datasets, one engine. Lending Club,

## Recording tips

- Read each scene aloud once before the take. The cadence should feel
  like a partner talking, not a narrator reading.
- The captions on screen are the headline. Your voice carries the
  *why* — the partner-grade context the captions don't have room for.
- Leave a half-second of silence at every scene boundary. The video
  has motion + caption transitions there; let them breathe.
- If you blow a scene, don't restart the whole take — you can drop in
  a single re-recorded clip per scene during edit, since the video has
  clear scene breaks.
